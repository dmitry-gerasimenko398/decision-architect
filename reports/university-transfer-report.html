Assumptions & audit trail

What this result depends on

User-confirmed assumptions

  • The postpone alternative is an approximate strategy over the full comparison horizon: remain in the current faculty for one semester, gather information, and then choose between staying and transferring. This multi-criteria model does not solve that staged future decision as a full sequential decision process.
  • Uncertainty for different criteria is sampled independently. This is an approximation and may not fully reflect real relationships between outcomes.
  • Value of additional information and reversibility represents only residual value not already reflected in subject interest, career prospects, graduation time, or compatibility with family responsibilities.
  • All three alternatives have been verified not to require relocation, regular overnight stays away from home, or mandatory placements that prevent regular returns home.
  • The four subjective criteria use ratings from 0 (worst meaningful outcome) to 10 (best meaningful outcome).
  • Time until graduation is measured in semesters, with 7 semesters as the worst meaningful anchor and 4 semesters as the best meaningful anchor.
  • One-at-a-time weight sensitivity preserves the relative proportions of other weights and does not model simultaneous changes to several preference weights.

Mathematical details

The report displays stored engine results; it does not run these calculations.

Anchor normalization:
utility = clamp((value − worst anchor) / (best anchor − worst anchor), 0, 1)

Weighted utility:
total utility = Σ (criterion weight × normalized criterion utility)

Triangular raw mean:
mean = (minimum + most likely + maximum) / 3

Monte Carlo ranking draws each confirmed triangular estimate with the recorded seed, calculates weighted utility, and ranks feasible alternatives by mean utility. Simulation ties split win credit.

One-at-a-time fixed-sample sensitivity:
w_target(x) = x
w_other(x) = original w_other × (1 − x) / (1 − original w_target)

The engine reuses stored criterion means, solves linear winner crossings analytically, and verifies both sides. It does not resample or change hard-constraint eligibility.
